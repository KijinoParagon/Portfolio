What is CBE?

CBE is Competency Based Education. CBE focuses on Mastery of a subject/content, not time spent on the subject/content.

How does CBE work for...?

The number one thing to note about CBE is that it's not perfect. If CBE doesn't work for one skill, then don't apply it. CBE is flexible. We use CBE where it counts.

These are my takeaways from CBExchange 2024:

Trends in Employment & Education Requirements

  • Shifts in Degree Requirements: 51% of Fortune 500 Companies have reduced degree requirements, seeking either degrees or equivalent experience.
  • Competency-Based Education (CBE) Demand: CBE benefits heavily from industry partnerships, and employers desire graduates who can perform on Day 1.
  • Economic Shift: The US is transitioning from a service-based to a digital economy, with implications for education and workforce readiness.
  • Upskilling Priority: Upskilling is crucial and often more cost-effective than hiring new employees.

Competency-Based Education (CBE) Model

  • Core Components: Competency = Knowledge + Skill + Behavior + Application.
  • CBE Structure: Non-traditional grading; focuses on mastery (e.g., a "C" may not be passing).
  • Human-Centered Assessment: CBE assessments should minimize bias and be focused on actual skills and competencies.
  • Equity and Accessibility: CBE should aim to be inclusive, offering equitable assessments and focusing on underprivileged students' needs.
  • Challenges in CBE: Retention, pacing, faculty workload, compliance, and peer support.
  • CBE Coaching: Faculty should act as CBE coaches, providing guidance on competencies rather than strict timelines.

Partnerships and Collaboration

  • Potential Partnerships: Explore partnerships with the National Governor's Association, CBEN, recruiters, and industries to create a graduate pipeline.
  • Industry Interaction: Connecting with industry leaders, HR conferences (e.g., SHRM), and involving recruiters for stronger student-industry alignment.
  • K-12 Engagement: Collaborate with K-12 education to build early positive connections.
  • Apprenticeships: Develop more apprenticeships, including registered apprenticeships, to enhance skill development and job readiness.

Student Support and Retention

  • Building Relationships: Peer learning, group projects, and open labs can help foster soft skills and connections.
  • 1:1 Meetings and Face-to-Face Interactions: Regular check-ins with new students can improve retention.
  • Support and Flexibility: Approaching students with offers of support rather than focusing on deadlines encourages openness and comfort.
  • Cultural and Social Development: Encourage community to address social isolation issues among adults.

Assessment and Skill Validation

  • Portfolio-Based Assessment: Tools like Valid-8 can be used to evaluate student portfolios as authentic assessments.
  • Assessing Beyond Traditional Metrics: Behavior-based personas over demographics; stories (qualitative data) along with stats are valuable.
  • Language Accessibility in Assessment: Consider non-English assessments for trades where language may not impact job performance.

Technological Integration and Innovation

  • Digital Tools: Utilize AI for simulated internships, competency mapping tools like the WGU Achievement Wallet for resume building.
  • Simulated Experiences: Create virtual internships or interview projects with industry representatives to help students gain experience.
  • Microcredentials: Microcredentials can increase momentum by showcasing specific skill achievements.

Faculty and Program Development

  • Workload and Contract Revisions: Faculty workload models need adjustments for CBE, potentially including team teaching.
  • Office Hours: Due to the more flexible nature of CBE, set office hours become less valuable, as student needs will shift.
  • Experimental Approach: Run pilot programs to test efficacy, with openness to iterative change.
  • Onboarding Focus: Smooth onboarding is essential, especially as students transition from traditional to CBE programs.

Competency-Based Education Challenges

  • Compliance: Regulatory compliance may limit flexibility in CBE programs.
  • Job Market Readiness: Emphasize resume keyword relevance to match employer scanning practices.
  • Momentum and Confidence: Momentum boosts confidence, which in turn fuels student persistence in programs.
  • Skill Gaps in Digital Literacy: Basic computing and cybersecurity skills are valuable across industries, alongside written/spoken language and AI literacy.

Future Program Opportunities

  • Specific Programs: Explore PACE programs for Cybersecurity, language, and AI literacy.
  • Faculty and K-12 Teacher Upskilling: Provide upskilling for educators to maintain relevance with emerging digital competencies.
  • Information Analyst Growth: Information Analyst roles are expected to grow significantly; consider data science-oriented programming for this need.

Keywords/Definitions/Glossary

Competency
The ability of an individual to complete a task. Measures not just how well they know a topic, but how well they could perform that task as a job duty.
Up Skill
Additional training given to those already in the field
For example, a company may come to a college and ask the college to train their employees to work with HTML/CSS/JS because they don't have any front end developers.
Registered Apprenticeship
An apprenticeship that has been validated and recognized by the Department of Labor, or another state body that works with apprenticeships/labor.
Micro Credential
A smaller credential, that isn't a degree, but certifies a skill.
For example, a SQL Certification
CPL - Credit for Prior Learning
Credit given based on competencies proven by assessment.
For example, if we assess that they have learned C# on their own, we should try to give them the opportunity to prove that to us and give them credit for it.
PLA - Prior Learning Assessment
Assessment of prior learning.
*See CPL - Credit for Prior Learning
CBE Coach
Staff or faculty member familiar with a program who will guide a student in picking the pacing/order that they complete competencies.
Peer Learning
Teaching method involving utilizing peer-to-peer relationships for students to gain insight that's more relatable to them, as well as reinforcement by answering more relevant questions from their peers.
